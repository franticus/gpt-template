Customize Your Cookie Settings

Cookies are small pieces of data sent from a website and stored on the user's computer by the web browser while it is running. The browser stores each message in a small file called a cookie. When you request another page from the server, your browser sends the cookie back to the server. Cookies have been developed as a reliable mechanism that allows websites to remember information or record user activity when browsing pages.

  Necessary ∨

Necessary cookies - typically, these cookies are essential for the basic functionality of the website. They assist in displaying relevant information, customizing user experiences, and implementing and maintaining security features. Generally, without these cookies, the website's operation is not possible, or its functionality may be severely impaired.

  Functional ∨

Functional cookies - typically, these cookies are employed to enhance the functional characteristics of your website, making it more user-friendly. These cookies remember user-selected settings (such as language and time zone settings), allowing users to avoid adjusting the settings each time they visit the site.

  Analytical ∨

Analytical cookies - typically, these cookies indicate whether the user has visited your site previously. Analytical cookies enable you to recognize and count the number of users on your site and understand how these users navigate through your site.

  Marketing ∨

Marketing, targeting, and advertising cookies - Usually, these cookies record visitors' visits to your site, the pages they visited, and the links they clicked on. This information is used to make the advertisements displayed on it more relevant to the interests of the user. Advertising cookies allow you to know if the user has already seen a specific advertisement or an advertisement of a certain type.
